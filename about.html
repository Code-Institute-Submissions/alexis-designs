About Batik

Batik is an artistic medium developed in the Malay and Indonesia archipelagos, originating from the island of Java hundreds of years ago. Traditionally a wax drawing is created on cloth and then dye is applied in the unwaxed spaces, sometimes with 10 or 20 applications of wax and different dyes. Eventually the wax is all removed leaving an intricate design with veins running through the cracks of the wax. Designs are, not unexpectedly, different throughout regions and in recent years, have been automated and so often "batik" designs have been created using the same printing methods as are available in the West.

Batik is most usually produced as a 2 metre long piece of fabric, a sarong and can either consist of one repeating pattern, or is divided up into several sections. There are slight differences, in general, between Malaysian and Indonesian batiks, with Malaysian pieces hosting lighter colours, and contain motifs more directed towards nature, in particular, leaves and flowers. Batik created in Java, where the technique came to life, as a whole uses deeper, more earthy colours, although different regions place emphasise on different themes and imagery.

No matter how the colourful fabrics are produced, the inspiration and lure is in the juxtaposition of colours, patterns and designs. In October 2009, UNESCO designated Indonesian batik as a Masterpiece of Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity

About Alexis

Alexis moved to East Asia in 1982 from Europe, she brought with her love of textiles. She has been lucky to be able to collect and use a variety of Asian textiles, in particular: batik. Living in Malaysia for the last 30 years, she has developed a love of bright colours that complements the vibrant tropical sunlight, that also brightens the decor of cooler, less sunny climates. She couples this passion for vibrancy with her dislike of waste, resulting in the beautiful batik patchwork found in her work.

She creates her pieces using handmade cotton fabrics in her home in Penang, Malaysia where she holds permenant residency with her husband of 36 years. Her compositions are immediately recognisable with the intricate designs, exquisite patterns and sense of style, using the beautiful fabrics that she sources personally from her local market.
